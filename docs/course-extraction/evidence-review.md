# Evidence Review — Rebuilding the 6-Week Postpartum Course
2026-07-13. What current evidence says, what the original course got right, and what the Lumora rebuild changes.

## What the original got right (keep)
- **Additive habit-based nutrition** (add berries, greens, nuts, seeds, protein, grains — not restriction). Behavior-change literature strongly favors small additive habits over restriction for adherence, and this framing protects milk supply.
- **Phase gating by consistency** ("don't advance until daily for 2 weeks") is sound habit formation.
- **Whole-food, fiber- and protein-forward choices** align with postpartum nutrient needs.
- **Home-or-gym dumbbell strength work, 3 days/week** is realistic for new moms; progressive overload via reps/sets is sound.
- **Medical disclaimer** present (keep and strengthen with symptom red flags).

## What must change (evidence-based corrections)

### 1. Timeline & clearance gating
- ACOG ([Committee Opinion 804](https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/committee-opinion/articles/2020/04/physical-activity-and-exercise-during-pregnancy-and-the-postpartum-period), [Exercise After Pregnancy FAQ](https://www.acog.org/womens-health/faqs/exercise-after-pregnancy)): resume gradually as soon as medically safe; start with pelvic floor work and walking within days if uncomplicated vaginal birth; cesarean or complications → clear with OB first.
- Rebuild: explicit "start here" gate — program assumes ~6-week medical clearance; C-section timelines called out.

### 2. High-impact work too early (biggest fix)
- Original has **jump squats in Week 1**. Evidence ([IJSPT return-to-sport timeline](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9528725/), [Pacer PT](https://www.pacerphysicaltherapy.com/blog/postpartum-running-should-i-run-with-diastasis-recti/)): high-impact before ~12 weeks postpartum risks pelvic floor dysfunction (high-impact exercise carries ~4.6× the pelvic floor dysfunction risk of low impact); return to impact only when symptom-free (no leaking, heaviness, pain) and after progressive strength building.
- Rebuild: Weeks 1–2 impact-free; impact introduced as an *optional* progression in Weeks 5–6 only with a symptom self-screen passed; every jump exercise gets a low-impact swap.

### 3. Missing core & pelvic floor foundation
- [Cochrane 2020](https://www.cochranelibrary.com/cdsr/doi/10.1002/14651858.CD007471.pub4/references) and a [2025 systematic review](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12013572/): postpartum pelvic floor muscle training reduces urinary incontinence odds ~37% and prolapse ~56%; transversus abdominis + PFM work reduces inter-rectus distance (diastasis recti).
- Rebuild: every week opens with breath + core + pelvic floor work (360° breathing, TA activation, kegels + relaxation); diastasis recti self-check taught in Week 1; "doming/leaking/heaviness = regress" rule throughout.

### 4. Nutrition targets for the postpartum/breastfeeding body
- Safe rate of loss while nursing: ~0.5–1 lb/week via modest ~300–500 kcal gap, not before ~2 months when supply is established; most nursing mothers need ≥1,800 kcal/day ([Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics](https://www.eatright.org/health/pregnancy/breastfeeding-and-formula/losing-weight-while-breastfeeding), [InfantRisk Center](https://www.infantrisk.com/content/weight-loss-while-breastfeeding-whats-safe-moms), [KellyMom](https://kellymom.com/nutrition/mothers-diet/mom-weightloss/)).
- Key lactation nutrients to feature in food choices: protein 1.3–1.8 g/kg, DHA 200–300 mg, choline 550 mg, iodine 290 mcg, iron, vitamin D ([Needed](https://thisisneeded.com/blogs/postpartum/what-to-look-for-in-a-postpartum-multivitamin), [Centrum](https://www.centrum.com/learn/articles/nutrients-and-routine/postnatal-vitamins-for-breastfeeding-mothers/)).
- Rebuild: keep the food-add phases but annotate *why* each food matters postpartum (e.g., salmon/chia → DHA; eggs → choline — note original used egg *whites*, dropping the choline-rich yolk; whole eggs recommended). Drop "fat-free/low-fat" diet-culture framing; fats support hormones and DHA.

### 5. Hydration target correction
- Original: "2 cups → eventually 12 cups." Evidence ([IOM via WebMD](https://www.webmd.com/baby/what-to-know-staying-hydrated-while-pregnant-breastfeeding), [Northwest Family Clinics](https://www.northwestfamilyclinics.com/blog/breastfeeding-and-hydration-how-much-water-do-you-actually-need)): total fluids ~13 cups (3.1 L) for lactating women, ~9 cups otherwise; thirst + pale-urine check is the practical guide.
- Rebuild: habit anchor "drink every time you nurse/feed" + realistic ramp to ~9–13 cups depending on nursing status.

### 6. Program balance
- Original trains only "arms" and "legs" (mostly isolation) — no rows until Week 2, no core, minimal posterior chain, no pushing/pulling balance for the "feeding posture" upper back.
- Rebuild: each strength day = squat/hinge + push/pull + carry/core pattern; keep her exercise vocabulary (glute bridge, rows, curls, lunges) so the 19 videos stay reusable.

## Exercise selection (2026-07-13 revision — full programming rebuild)
The workout plan was rebuilt from the rehab literature rather than re-skinning the original isolation split:
- **Daily pelvic floor protocol ("The Daily Dose")**: 3×8 max contractions, 6 s hold / 6 s rest, plus 10 quick flicks, daily ≥3 months, positions progressing lying→sitting→standing — the dosage used in the PFMT trials ([supervised PFMT RCT protocol](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10151990/), [Cochrane 2020](https://www.cochranelibrary.com/cdsr/doi/10.1002/14651858.CD007471.pub4/references)).
- **Phased progression per the [IJSPT return-to-sport timeline](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9528725/)** (Selman 2022): low-load activation (TA holds, pelvic tilts, heel slides, bridges, clamshells, sit-to-stand, calf raises, short walks) → loaded functional strength 8–12 reps (squat, step-up, lunges incl. lateral/reverse, hinge) → power/impact drills only after the run-readiness benchmarks (1-min wall sit/plank/step-ups/squats, 10 single-leg calf raises & squats, hopping symptom-free, ≥12 weeks pp).
- **Diastasis recti work** from the DR trials ([12-week intervention](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8136546/), [TA vs RA RCT](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10686295/)): deep-core holds with position progression, dead bug, bird dog (+crunch), side plank progression, glute bridges; doming = regress rule throughout.
- **Functional patterns over isolation**: every session = squat/hinge + push/pull + carry (suitcase/farmer carries for anti-lean core, RDL for car-seat lifting mechanics, rows/presses for feeding posture). Original bicep/tricep work kept only as an optional 2×12 finisher.
- **Jog re-introduction** (optional, Week 6+): 1 min jog / 2 min walk intervals on slight incline, +1 min per week if symptom-free — the IJSPT walk-jog protocol.

## Success-rate framing for the sales page / intro
Honest, evidence-aligned promises: consistent strength + habit programs in the first postpartum year improve continence odds, core function, energy, and sustainable ~0.5–1 lb/week loss — not "drop the baby weight in 6 weeks."
