# The Lumora Method: 12-Week Container

Your operating manual for every 1:1 client. Same shape every time. Repeatable, predictable, and built so you don't burn out.

---

## What This Is

A 12-week private wellness mentorship for the woman who is done with generic advice and ready for a healing experience built around her.

She gets:
- A custom meal plan, rebuilt by you, every week
- One 30-minute call with you each week
- Async messaging access (24-hour reply window, Mon–Fri)
- A weekly teaching drop on the topic for that phase
- A final maintenance plan she can run on her own

**Outcome:** she leaves knowing how to feed herself for life. Not on a diet. Not counting forever. Eating in a way that fits her body, her season, and her real schedule.

---

## The Five Phases

### Phase 1 — Ground (Weeks 1–2): Foundation
**Goal:** she eats from a plan made for her body.

- Onboarding deep dive (form already in your admin)
- Baseline measurements: weight, waist, hips, energy, sleep, mood
- First custom meal plan delivered by end of week 1
- **Teaching drop:** "Macros 101: what they are, why they matter, why calories in / calories out misses the point"

### Phase 2 — Rhythm (Weeks 3–5): Daily Patterns
**Goal:** her day starts feeding her instead of draining her.

- Adjust plan based on weeks 1–2 logs (what she actually ate, energy patterns, fullness)
- Layer in meal timing rules
- Sleep + stress check-in
- **Teaching drops:**
  - Week 3: "Cortisol and Coffee: why you eat before you caffeinate"
  - Week 4: "Morning Sun and Body Clocks: cheap tools that change everything"
  - Week 5: "Your Energy Window: how to time your biggest meal"

### Phase 3 — Heal (Weeks 6–8): Food Quality
**Goal:** she chooses ingredients with reasons.

- Rebuild plan around what is working
- First measurement check-in vs. baseline
- Layer in cycle awareness (or postpartum awareness if applicable)
- **Teaching drops:**
  - Week 6: "Real Food vs. Processed: what your liver sees"
  - Week 7: "Insulin and the Mother's Body: why weight holds on"
  - Week 8: "Your Hormones Across the Month: feeding each phase"

### Phase 4 — Integrate (Weeks 9–11): Real Life
**Goal:** she can do this without you.

- She drafts her own week's plan, you review and refine
- Troubleshoot what is stuck (plateaus, social pressure, busy weeks)
- Eating out, travel, kids' birthdays, the all-day work meeting
- **Teaching drops:**
  - Week 9: "Eating Out Without Starting Over"
  - Week 10: "Travel, Disruption, and the Reset Day"
  - Week 11: "Plateaus: read your body, not your scale"

### Phase 5 — Graduate (Week 12): Handoff
**Goal:** she leaves with a maintenance plan and a clear next step.

- Final measurements
- Reflection conversation: what changed, what she is keeping
- Maintenance plan delivered
- Renewal offer (continued 1:1 at a maintenance rate) OR step-down to The Lumora Method course + community
- Ask for a testimonial and case-study release

## The Weekly Rhythm

The same shape every week so you do not have to think.

| Day | What you do | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Sunday eve | Review her week's logs, prep her plan | 60–90 min |
| Monday | Send weekly meal plan + teaching drop | 15 min |
| Mon or Tue | 30-min coaching call | 30 min |
| Wed–Fri | Async messages, answer questions | ~30 min total |
| Friday | Quick "how was the week" prompt to her | 5 min |

**Total per client per week: ~2 to 2.5 hours.** At 5 clients that is 10–13 focused hours, and the rest of your week is yours.

## What You Write Once and Reuse Forever

The teaching drops are the leverage. Write each one as a short note (300–600 words) or a 5–10 minute Loom video. Once written, every client gets the same one on the same week of their container.

Drops to write (in priority order, while you take your first clients):
1. Macros 101
2. Cortisol and Coffee
3. Real Food vs. Processed
4. Eating Out Without Starting Over
5. Morning Sun and Body Clocks
6. Insulin and the Mother's Body
7. Your Energy Window
8. Hormones Across the Month
9. Travel and the Reset Day
10. Plateaus
11. The Maintenance Plan
12. The Final Reflection

You can finish all twelve in roughly 12–15 hours of writing. That is the foundation of the course later, too.
