Phase 3 · Hydrate & Sustain

The simplest phase, and the one most women are furthest from.

Water is unglamorous, so it gets skipped. But mild dehydration reads as fatigue, headaches, brain fog, and cranky digestion, which is everything the newborn season already serves you for free. And if you're nursing, roughly 87% of what you make every day is water.

Your targets

Not nursing

About 9 cups (2.2 L) of fluids a day

Nursing

About 13 cups (3.1 L) of fluids a day

"Fluids" counts everything: water, milk, soup, herbal tea. Don't force chugging; build the habit and let thirst do the fine-tuning. Easiest self-check: your urine should run pale yellow.

Make it automatic

  • Anchor it to the baby: every time you sit down to nurse or give a bottle, you drink too. Feeds per day ≈ glasses per day. Done.
  • Station bottles where life happens: nursing chair, kitchen counter, stroller, car.
  • Start with two: if you're currently at nearly nothing, begin with two cups a day and add one every few days until you reach your target.
  • Make it nice: lemon, cucumber, mint, fizzy water. Flavor counts.

Caffeine: you don't have to give up coffee. Up to ~300 mg a day (about two mugs) is considered fine while nursing. Just don't let coffee replace water.

Fourteen days to make it stick

Tick a box each day you hit your fluid target. Two weeks of ticks and the habit mostly runs itself.

Sustaining all three phases

Put the phases side by side and you can see what they add up to: berries + greens + nuts + seeds + a palm of protein + whole grains + water is a complete template. Look at your plate: half colorful plants, a palm of protein, a fist of whole grains or starchy veg, a thumb of healthy fat, water beside it. That's the Lumora plate, no tracking app required.

This phase never ends. It just becomes how you live. Repeat any phase any time life knocks a habit loose (travel, teething, growth spurts; it happens to everyone).
