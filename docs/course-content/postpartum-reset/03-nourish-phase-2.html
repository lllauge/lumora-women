Phase 2 · Power Up Your Plate

Keep everything from Phase 1. Now we add the recovery heavyweights: omega-3s, protein, and fiber-rich carbs.

Phase 1 is running on autopilot? Beautiful. Phase 2 adds the three nutrients that do the heaviest lifting in postpartum recovery. If you're nursing, they are also the ones your baby is drawing from you daily.

Daily add № 4
A spoonful of omega-3 seeds

Add 1–3 tablespoons of chia, hemp, or ground flax seeds daily.

  • Yogurt
  • Smoothies
  • Oatmeal
  • Soups & salads
  • Chia pudding

Why it matters now: these seeds carry plant omega-3s plus minerals that support mood and hormone balance in the postpartum window.

If you're nursing: your baby's brain is built partly from the DHA in your milk. Seeds help, but the richest sources are fatty fish. Aim for salmon or sardines twice a week, or ask your provider about a 200–300 mg DHA supplement.

Daily add № 5
A palm of protein, at every meal

Add a palm-sized portion of protein to each meal (not just once a day; your muscles and healing tissues are asking all day long).

  • Whole eggs
  • Chicken
  • Salmon & white fish
  • Greek yogurt
  • Cottage cheese
  • Tofu & tempeh
  • Lentils & chickpeas
  • Beans
  • Shrimp
  • Lean beef

Why it matters now: protein is the raw material of recovery, and it's what makes the strength plan actually build strength. Rough target: 1.3–1.8 g per kg of body weight per day, which for most women simply means "a palm at every meal, plus a protein-ish snack."

Notice we say whole eggs, not egg whites. The yolk holds the choline (you need ~550 mg/day while nursing) and most of the vitamins. And full-fat yogurt is welcome here. The fat keeps you full and your hormones fed.

Daily add № 6
Carbs that hold you over

Add ½–1 cup of whole grains or fiber-rich starches to your meals. Whole grains are the workhorse here, but they're not the only option.

  • Oats
  • Quinoa
  • Brown rice
  • Whole-wheat bread
  • Farro
  • Bulgur
  • Buckwheat
  • Millet
  • Sweet potato
  • Butternut squash
  • Potatoes, skin on
  • Corn & peas

Why it matters now: steady carbohydrates are your energy floor, and the fiber that comes with them keeps blood sugar (and mood) from crashing mid-afternoon and postpartum digestion moving. They feed your workouts and support milk supply. Cutting carbs while nursing is one of the fastest ways to tank both energy and supply.

Three weeks, then Phase 3

You're now carrying six small habits. Give them three weeks to settle in. That's long enough for this to stop feeling like a program and start feeling like how you eat. Tick a box each day all six adds happen.

Stay here until the Phase 1 + Phase 2 adds have been daily habits for at least three weeks. Then Phase 3 is the easiest one of all.
