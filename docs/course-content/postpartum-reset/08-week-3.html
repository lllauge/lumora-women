Week 3 · Build

Heavier dumbbells, single-leg work, and your deep core moves to hands-and-knees.

Now we build. Four sets on the big movements, weights that make the last reps honest, and your first single-leg strength work: the balance and hip stability that carrying a baby on one hip demands daily.

Deep-core holds move to hands-and-knees this week (in the Foundation Five below). Gravity makes your core work harder for the same gentle draw-in.

Start every workout: The Foundation Five (~5 minutes)

  1. 360° breathing: 5 slow breaths. Inhale into your ribs and belly, exhale fully and feel your deep core gently wrap.
  2. Pelvic tilts: 10 slow rocks. Flatten your lower back toward the floor on the exhale, release on the inhale.
  3. Deep-core holds: 5 × 5-second holds, on hands and knees. Exhale, gently draw your lower belly in (about 30% effort, a hug rather than a crunch), and breathe while you hold.
  4. Glute bridge: 2 × 10. Exhale up, squeeze at the top, lower with control.
  5. Bird dog: 2 × 6 per side. Long spine, no wobble, opposite arm and leg reach.

This is your warm-up and your deep-core rebuild. Do not skip it. It is the most important five minutes of the program.

Rest 60–90 seconds between sets. Leave at least one day between workouts. Finish every session with a 5–10 minute walk or other gentle cardio you enjoy.

Finisher: 5–10 minutes. Try intervals: 1 minute brisk, 1 minute easy.

Optional arm finisher (if you have time and energy left): bicep curls + overhead tricep extensions, 2 × 12 each. Nice to have. The main work above is what changes your body.

Nutrition: begin Phase 2 this week: seeds, a palm of protein at every meal, fiber-rich carbs. The protein especially matters now that training is heavier.
