The Lumora Postpartum Reset

Week 5 · Progress

Peak strength, a taste of power — and, if your body says yes, the first impact.

Two things happen this week: your deep-core work moves to standing (the hardest position), and impact becomes available — optional, earned, and only after the readiness check below. Plenty of strong women skip impact entirely this round and repeat the program with it next time. Both paths are wins.

The impact-readiness check

Borrowed from the return-to-running criteria physical therapists use. Attempt impact only if every line is true, with no leaking, heaviness, pain, or doming:

  • You're at least 12 weeks postpartum
  • No symptoms in any workout so far, or in the 48 hours after
  • 1 minute each, symptom-free: wall sit · plank (knees fine) · step-ups · bodyweight squats
  • 10 single-leg calf raises per side
  • 10 single-leg squats to a chair per side
  • 30 seconds of fast marching, then 10 small hops in place

Any box unchecked? Use the low-impact swap (squat to calf raise) — it builds the same power, and the box will check itself in time.

Start every workout: The Foundation Five (~5 minutes)

  1. 360° breathing — 5 slow breaths. Inhale into your ribs and belly, exhale fully and feel your deep core gently wrap.
  2. Pelvic tilts — 10 slow rocks. Flatten your lower back toward the floor on the exhale, release on the inhale.
  3. Deep-core holds — 5 × 5-second holds, standing tall. Exhale, gently draw your lower belly in (about 30% effort — a hug, not a crunch), breathe while you hold.
  4. Glute bridge — 2 × 10. Exhale up, squeeze at the top, lower with control.
  5. Bird dog — 2 × 6 per side. Long spine, no wobble, opposite arm and leg reach.

This is your warm-up and your deep-core rebuild. Do not skip it — it is the most important five minutes of the program.

Rest 60–90 seconds between sets. Leave at least one day between workouts. Finish every session with a 5–10 minute walk or other gentle cardio you enjoy.

Finisher: 5–10 minutes; if you passed the check, you may swap 1–2 of those minutes for light jogging on a slight incline.

Your body sets the pace. If you notice any of these during or after exercise, drop back to the easier version and give it more time:

  • Leaking urine, or a feeling of heaviness / dragging in your pelvis
  • Doming or coning down the middle of your belly
  • Pain, or bleeding that returns or gets heavier after a workout

These are signals, not failures. If they persist, a pelvic-floor physical therapist is worth their weight in gold.
